The Water Cycle

Up in the air,
cooler temperatures cause
the vapor to condence(vapor
turning back into liquid).

Clouds are formed
by the wind and air moving
the moisture around.

With time the clouds
become heavier with water. They are then
rain bearing clouds.

The water
then falls
from the sky
in the form
of rain, snow
or sleet.

As the rain falls to the ground it forms puddles, streams and rivers on the surface. This is by the natural force of gravity.

Water is also absorbed into the soil.
Sometimes water in the ground moves up
to the surface and can evaporate or
run back into rivers and streams.

The lakes and oceans have a larger surface
area to absorb the suns energy (heat) which
warms the surfaces.

As the water heats up it evaporates, turning a liquid into a vapor.

Green plants (forsts and all vegetaion) also release
moisture into the air in a process called transpiration.

The rising air lifts the vapor high up into the air.
Where it builds up in the clouds and starts the
process all over again.
